Mumbai Special

Colaba is Mumbai’s heritage district and when you pull up, you can definitely see its colonial past seeping through.The Gateway is striking and the second most photographed attraction in India.Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus is a UNESCO world heritage site and one grandest train stations built by the British.Prince of Wales museum is actually interesting!

You can indulge in contemporary delicacies at upscale restaurants like Olive bar & Cafe where you can even find ranbir kapoor, KJO in Hakkasan, Amitabh Bachhan in 212 Bar & Grill, Shahid Kapoor, Katrina kaif & Shradhha Kapoor in the Farmer. Also Soho House is not just a regular café or restaurant but an 11-storey building which has its own roof-top pool.

gra finds a mention in the epic Mahabharata, where it is referred to as ‘Agraban’ or an integral part of the Braj Bhumi or the land of Lord Krishna. A lot of significant historical events in the city are said to have unfolded during the reign of Raja Badal Singh, a Sikarwar Rajput king, who is believed to have founded the city in 1475.

The only place you should consider staying is at the Taj Mahal Palace.Not only is it right by the Gateway of India but also it’s one of the most iconic hotels in the country.Being a 5 star “Heritage Grand” class hotel, it has attracted a wealth of stars through its doors over the years. Queen Elizabeth II, Amitabh Bachchan, Obama and even John Lennon have all checked in to experience the luxury.
